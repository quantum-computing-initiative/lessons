Superposition & Phase

Information from this section comes from Qiskit Textbook. Single Qubit Gates.

Hadamard Gate

$$ H = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} $$

$$H\vert 0\rangle = \vert+\rangle$$ $$H\vert 1\rangle = \vert-\rangle$$

Apply H to |0> and |1>. What is the difference? Show math on iPad

The + and - have to do with the sign of the phase (we'll see this on the bloch sphere)

$R_\phi$ Gate

$$ R_\phi = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\phi} \end{bmatrix} $$

Performs a rotation by $\phi$ radians on the Z axis.

What gate does this resemble?

$S$/$S\dagger$ ($\sqrt{Z}$/$\sqrt{Z}^\dagger$) Gates

$$ \sqrt{Z} = S = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{\frac{i\pi}{2}} \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \sqrt{Z}^\dagger = S^\dagger = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{-\frac{i\pi}{2}} \end{bmatrix} $$

Special case of the $R_\phi$ gate with $\phi = \frac{\pi}{2}$

Why is this called $sqrt{Z}$? (applying this gate twice results in Z)

Are they their own inverses? (No. Show math on Jamboard)

$T/T^\dagger$ ($\sqrt[4]{Z}/\sqrt[4]{Z}^\dagger$) Gates

$$ T = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{\frac{i\pi}{4}} \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ T^\dagger = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{-\frac{i\pi}{4}} \end{bmatrix} $$
